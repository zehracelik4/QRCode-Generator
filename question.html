WHAT'S QR CODE?

A QR code, or Quick Response code is a type of two-dimensional barcode that can store various types of information in a square pattern of black and white modules. QR codes were first developed in Japan in 1994 and have become widely used for encoding data that can be quickly scanned and read by smartphones, tablets, and other devices with cameras.

QR codes can store different types of data, including URLs (web addresses), text, contact information (vCards), and more. When a QR code is scanned using a compatible device, the encoded information is quickly read and processed, often leading to actions like opening a website, displaying text, or adding contact information to the device's address book.

They are often used for marketing purposes, product labeling, sharing contact information, linking to websites, and providing quick access to information without the need to manually type or enter data.

To use QR codes, you typically need a smartphone or a device with a camera and a QR code reader app. Many modern smartphone cameras have built-in QR code scanning functionality, eliminating the need for a separate app. Just point your camera at the QR code, and if it's recognized, your device will prompt you to take an action based on the encoded information.
